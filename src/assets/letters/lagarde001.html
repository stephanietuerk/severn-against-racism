 Wednesday, June 3, 2020
 
Headmaster Lagarde sent the following letter to parents and faculty on Monday, June 1 and would like to share it with the broader Severn community. 
 
 
June 1, 2020
 
Dear Severn Community,
 
I’ve often used the opportunity of convocation at the beginning of each year to emphasize aspects of Severn’s mission.

Character, Conduct, Scholarship:

Character is the way in which one engages with others and the communities of which they are a part. Without character our mission is a worthless group of words; without character the fabric of our community would come unraveled; without character Severn would cease to be the special community that it is. Why is character first in our mission, because everything hinges on your character.
 

Marshal the Courage to Lead:

The choice not to act is itself an act. Sometimes, failing to act is just as blameworthy as acting badly. If you have the power to help someone, if you have the ability to right a wrong, or if you have the opportunity to make your voice heard, you should rightfully feel bad when you fail to do so.

Pay attention – recognize something should be done – gather or muster your courage, and then take action and by doing so, you lead. Throughout your life there will always be critics on the sidelines tearing people down. The sidelines are not where you want to live your life. The Severn community needs your energy, your passion, your impatience for progress. Don’t shrink from a feared situation, particularly if you know the right thing to do is take action, to lead. If we are to be and remain a great school, Severn needs you to marshal the courage to lead.
 
Over the past few days, I’ve struggled with what to, or even if I should, write this letter. I’ve not struggled with condemning the injustice perpetrated upon mistreated, underserved and marginalized communities, or acknowledging the anger and indignation of African-Americans who justly feel the pain and anguish of systemic racism. My reticence stems from the juxtaposition of celebrating Severn’s Class of 2020 last Friday, and the celebrations of our 5th and 8th grades as they promote to Middle and Upper School this week. In addition, the circumstances created by the COVID-19 pandemic renders face-to-face, intimate conversations with colleagues and students nearly impossible. Zoom is simply an imperfect medium on which to have such emotionally important conversations. Regardless, I hope that we can honor our students at this celebratory time in school, and also honor our moral responsibility to be engaged citizens, to marshal the courage to lead and act with character.
 
We are experiencing a significant moment in our lives and, likely, a defining moment in the lives of the young people we all help to grow up to be passionate, principled women and men of character. Already, in a variety of ways, Severn faculty and staff have reached out to each other and to students to help process their own reactions to these tragic events, and will continue to do so.
 
With all that is going on in the world today, it may feel virtually impossible to say anything useful right now. I ask all of you, though, to not just weather the storm, but have conversations with your children, help them learn from this horrific situation and help move our community forward. We can make progress together.
 
I have the utmost faith in this community to not only ask difficult questions but seek answers and then act. We can help make change. We can make a difference together.
 
Be well,
 
Doug Lagarde
