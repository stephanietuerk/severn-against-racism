Monday, June 8, 2020
Dear Alumni,
 
The following is a response by the Executive Committee of the Board of Trustees and Executive Administration sent to the authors of an open letter posted online expressing concern about the letter Mr. Lagarde sent to Severn families on June 1st and to the alumni body on June 3rd.
 
A number of alumni have signed onto the letter, and we want to make sure all alumni are aware of the School’s response.
 
------
 
We at Severn are angered and dismayed by racial injustice of any kind. We are particularly mindful of and condemn both the recent violence and the undeniable systemic racism against the black community, and recognize the opportunity Severn has to help prevent it.
 
We have made progress at Severn to create a more diverse and inclusive community but have significant work ahead of us. Without hesitation, Severn School’s leadership and the Board of Trustees commit to further progress. As we work toward that progress, we are grateful for your feedback and hope for your continued participation.
 
When we can gather as a community again in the fall, we will bring together alumni, students, faculty and parents to have constructive conversations about and develop action steps for ways Severn can achieve its goal of being a more diverse and inclusive community.
 
We appreciate your support of Severn School, its mission and its desire to improve and look forward to working together towards the improvements we agree are needed.
