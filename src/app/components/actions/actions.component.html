This group was formed in response to a letter that Head of School Doug Lagarde shared with the Severn Community on June 3, 2020. Sent at a time when American institutions from schools to corporations were communicating support for Black communities, condemning police brutality, and stating that Black Lives Matter, Mr. Lagarde's email did none of these things. That his letter mentioned issues of racial injustice only as an aside made many of us very concerned about the administration's commitment to Black students and to rooting out racism at the school.

As we connected with others, we discovered we were not alone in wanting to see Severn take a strong stand against racism as well as other types of discrimination. In response to Mr. Lagarde's letter, we wrote an open letter to the school that over 750 alumni, students, parents, and former faculty signed.

Through the discussions that followed Mr. Lagarde's letter and our initial response to it, we have heard more and more stories about racist and bigoted incidents occurring at the school in recent years and how the administration failed to address them.. We acknowledge the steps Severn has committed to taking and we will know that its Black students matter when students and their families feel supported and protected.
